Post 1 Veggie Fajita Bowl:

  • Total Time : 45 mins
  • Course: Main dish
  • Servings: 4
  • Author: Priya Chandra

Ingredients:

For the fajita veggies:
  1. 1 Tbsp olive oil
  2. 2 yellow onions sliced
  3. 1/2 of a medium jalapeo diced (omit if sensitive to spice; use a whole jalapeo if you like a lot of spice)
  4. 3 multicolored bell peppers seeded and sliced
  5. 8 oz mushrooms sliced
  6. 1 tsp minced garlic about one clove
  7. 1/2 tsp cumin
  8. 1/2 tsp chili powder
  9. 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  10. Sea salt to taste
  11. Juice of 1/2 a small lime
    12. For the spinach
  12. 1 Tbsp olive oil
  13. 2 tsp minced garlic about 4 cloves
  14. 8 oz fresh spinach
  15. 1/4 tsp crushed red pepper
  16. Sea salt to taste
  17. Splash of lime juice
    18. For the cilantro lime cauliflower rice
  18. 1 Tbsp olive oil
  19. 1 Tbsp diced onion
  20. 1/2 tsp minced garlic about one clove
  21. 12 oz bag frozen riced cauliflower
  22. 1/4 tsp cumin
  23. Sea salt to taste
  24. Chopped cilantro to taste
  25. Lime juice to taste
    26. To top
  26. Shredded cheddar to taste
  27. 1-2 avocados diced

Directions:

  1. Start with the fajita veggies.
  2. For the fajita veggies: Slice your veggies. Heat olive oil in a 12" cast iron skillet over medium-high heat for one minute, then add the onions and jalapeno. Cook over medium-high heat for two minutes, then add in the bell peppers.
  3. Continue to cook for three minutes, stirring occasionally, then stir in the mushrooms, garlic, cumin, chili powder, and smoked paprika. Continue cooking veggies over medium-high heat for about another seven minutes, stirring occasionally, then stir in the sea salt and lime juice and cook for three more minutes.
  4. While the fajita veggies are cooking, also saute your spinach and cook your cauliflower rice.
  5. For the spinach: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat for one minute. Add garlic and saute for 30 seconds, then add in the spinach, crushed red pepper, sea salt, and lime juice. Cook over medium heat for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until spinach is wilted. Remove spinach to a separate bowl.
  6. For the rice: After removing the sauteed spinach from the pan, add olive oil to the same large skillet. Saute onion and garlic over medium heat for one minute, then add in the bag of frozen cauliflower rice and cover the pan. Continue to cook covered over medium heat for seven minutes, stirring occasionally and breaking up clumps as you go.
  7. Once the riced cauliflower is heated through and browned, stir in the cumin, cilantro, sea salt, and lime juice.
  8. Now, assemble your fajita bowls!
  9. Divide the finished cilantro lime cauliflower rice among four bowls, then top each fajita bowl with spinach, fajita veggies, diced avocado, and shredded cheese.
  10. Garnish with additional cilantro, if desired. Mix everything up together burrito bowl style, and enjoy!
    11. Recipe Notes
  11. If you would instead like to use fresh cauliflower that you have riced using a food processor or box grater, go for it -- here, I just used a handy bag of frozen riced cauliflower.

Post 2 Buffalo Chicken with cauliflower

  • Total Time : 45 mins
  • Course: Main dish
  • Servings: 2
  • Author: Priya Chandra

Ingredients:

  1. extra-virgin olive oil, for pan
  2. 1 Tyson Cornish Hen
  3. 3/4 tsp. chili powder
  4. Kosher Salt
  5. Freshly ground black pepper
  6. 1 head of cauliflower
  7. crumbled blue cheese, for garnish
    8. FOR BUFFALO SAUCE
  8. 1/2 c. hot sauce, such as Franks Red Hot
  9. 1/4 c. melted butter
  10. 1 tbsp. Sriracha
  11. 1 1/2 tsp. honey
  12. 2 cloves garlic,inced
  13. 2 dashes of worcestershire sauce

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425º and brush a medium sheet pan with oil.
  2. To spatchcock the hen: Place the breast side down and cut out the backbone and discard. Flip the bird over and press down firmly on the breastbone to flatten. Place the bird on an prepared sheet pan skin side up, rub the skin all over with the chili powder, and season the entire bird with salt and pepper.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk to combine all buffalo sauce ingredients.
  4. Cut up half a head of cauliflower into medium to large florets, toss with a bit of olive oil, salt, pepper, a couple spoonfuls of the buffalo sauce, and set aside. Spoon some buffalo sauce over the Cornish hen and place it in the oven.
  5. Set a timer for 15 minutes, spoon more sauce over the bird and add the cauliflower to the sheet pan.
  6. Cook for about 30 minutes more (depending on the size of the bird) turning the cauliflower once and re-basting the bird. Once it is cooked through to an internal temperature of at least 165º remove from oven and serve with crumbled blue cheese.

Post 3 Chicken Stuffed Baked Avocados

  • Total Time : 18 mins
  • Course: Snack
  • Servings: 8
  • Author: Priya Chandra

Ingredients:

  1. 4 avocados, halved and pitted
  2. 2 cooked chicken breasts, shredded
  3. 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  4. ¼ cup chopped tomatoes
  5. ¼ teaspoon salt
  6. ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  7. 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  8. ½ cup shredded Parmesan cheese, or more to taste.

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  2. Scoop out some of the flesh in the center of each avocado; place into mixing bowl. Add chicken, cream cheese, tomatoes, salt, pepper, and cayenne pepper; mix well to combine. Scoop spoonfuls of chicken mixture into the wells of each avocado; top each with generous amount of Parmesan cheese. Place avocado halves, face-up, in muffin cups to stabilize.
  3. Bake avocados in preheated oven until cheese is melted, 8 to 10 minutes.
